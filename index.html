Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why do glucose levels in the body fluctuate, and what factors can contribute to these fluctuations?

    Glucose levels can vary due to factors like food intake, physical activity, stress, and medications. Monitoring these factors helps understand and manage blood sugar levels.

  • How does an increase in glucose levels affect the body, and what are the potential health risks?

    Elevated glucose levels over time can lead to diabetes and increase the risk of heart disease, nerve damage, and other complications. Regular monitoring and lifestyle adjustments are crucial for maintaining optimal health.

  • What are the consequences of low glucose levels, and how can they be managed?

    Low blood sugar can cause dizziness, fatigue, and confusion. It's important to consume a balanced diet, monitor glucose levels regularly, and adjust medications under medical guidance to prevent hypoglycemia.

  • Why is creatinine important for kidney health, and how does it relate to overall well-being?

    Creatinine is a waste product filtered by the kidneys. Elevated levels may indicate kidney dysfunction. Monitoring creatinine levels helps assess kidney health, crucial for maintaining the body's balance and removing waste.

  • How does high creatinine levels in the blood relate to kidney health, and what implications does it have for overall well-being?

    Elevated creatinine levels may indicate impaired kidney function, suggesting potential kidney damage. Monitoring creatinine levels is essential for assessing kidney health and taking preventive measures to maintain overall well-being.
